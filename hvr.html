Problem



Opendoor's core product serves homeowners who are looking to sell their home by offering an instant cash value. However, most homeowners are not active sellers and are simply monitoring the value of their homes, willing to sell if the market yields high returns.

Solution



To better serve all homeowners, our team created the Opendoor Home Value Report. It consumerizes Opendoor's vast amount of real estate data to give users information about their home's valuation, neighborhood comparisons, and market trends – for free.

My Role & Process



As the sole product designer, I led the end-to-end research and design process. Due to scarce resources, I also rolled up my sleeves and coded some of the front end.

  • Competitive Research


    Investigated Zillow's Zestimate, Trulia home values, Redfin value estimator, etc.

  • Brainstorm Sessions &
    Design Principles


    Led sessions with PM, Eng, Design, Marketing and Ops to diverge and converge on ideas.

  • Information Architecture


    Low fidelity flows to illustrate experience and story at a high level.

  • Customer interviews


    Quick user research with concept mocks to validate the product direction.

  • High fidelity mocks


    Final mocks for responsive design were created in Sketch and exported to Zeplin.

  • Evaluative Research


    Tested with 10 users on usertesting.com for qualitative feedback to inform v2.

Impact



This product was designed and shipped within two months. Since launch, it has increased lead generation for Opendoor’s main product, and reduced the cost for each lead by almost 50%. Due to very positive responses from users, iteration and expansion is a top company priority for 2017.
